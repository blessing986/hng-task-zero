Areas of Low Confidence

Growth comes from acknowledging our weaknesses and working to improve them. Being honest about areas where I lack confidence is the first step toward mastery. Here are the skills I'm actively working to strengthen:

  • Backend Integration: While I'm comfortable with frontend development, working with APIs, managing authentication, and understanding backend architectures are areas where I need more practice and confidence.
  • Testing and Test-Driven Development: Writing comprehensive unit tests and integration tests is something I know is crucial but haven't fully mastered yet. I want to become proficient in testing frameworks and methodologies.
  • State Management in Complex Applications: Managing state across large-scale applications with multiple components and data flows still challenges me, and I want to gain deeper expertise in this area.
  • Performance Optimization: While I understand the basics, advanced performance tuning, profiling, and optimization techniques are skills I'm eager to develop further.

I believe that acknowledging these gaps is not a weakness but a strength. Each area I'm less confident in represents an opportunity for growth, and I'm committed to transforming these challenges into competencies through dedicated learning and practice.
